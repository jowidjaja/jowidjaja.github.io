A year ago, my apartment had a roach infestation. To my growing horror, I discovered an increasing number of baby roaches absent-mindedly scuttling their way across my wooden floors. A few clues pointed me to the potential location of the roach spawner. First, there was increase in traffic congestion around the cabinet underneath the kitchen sink. Second, the habitat in that very cabinet was prime real estate for a small roach family to invest in a little home. Turns out, this city does not accommodate unauthorized tenants, so the choice was theirs: leave or die. 

The roaches banded forces and decided to defend their territory. Little did they know, the switch had already been flipped. Defensive tactics had now turned offensive, and extermination was in order. The first strike was to snipe on sight. Every brave roach who dared enter the visual plane would be executed with deadly precision. Failure of this tactic quickly ensued due to the unforseen crippling fear of the snipee at executing such commands.  

A new strategy developed: infiltrate from the inside. Traps were set and bait was placed. All that was left was to wait and watch. The poison slowly infiltrated the colony in a course of months. To my excitement, the numbers began dwindle. 

The next phase consisted of the tedious removal the affected bodies. First, I began with the easier, less affected regions of the kitchen, emptying their belongings and wiping away any last traces of the wounded soldiers. Finally, I made my way to the dreaded sink cabinet, only to realize that while it was a prominent outpost, it was not the main base. 

Over months of deep cleaning, searching, and scavenging, the true source finally revealed itself when moving my fridge. At last, the stronghold was found. There, clustered under the warmth of the fridge compressor, lay the remnants of the colony, already seemingly affected by the poisonous bait released months earlier. 

Today, the bait remains in place for any stragglers who choose to pass by, a symbol of their fate if they choose to attempt unauthorized settlement. 

--- 
<br>

All jokes aside, this invasion was a profound experience largely because of its timing within my own healing journey. Through this experience, I found that the strategy for healing was remarkably similar to that of exterminating the roach colony. 

At times, roaches would randomly appear, much like unhealthy habits, intrusive thoughts, and coping mechanisms. My first instinct in both cases was to eliminate the visual problem immediately, but much like my experience with the roaches, sniping on sight resulted in extraordinary failure as it never really targeted the root cause. Instead, I had to allow the roaches to live long enough for the poison to take affect. Likewise, I had to spend a lot of time allowing unsightly parts of myself to exist while God quietely worked underneath the surface. 

I also noticed how both events unfolded over the course of months. Although some days were definitely more eventful than others, neither event was some dramatic breakthrough. Progress came through taking small actions each day and addressing one layer at a time which slowly built up my tolerance for handling the creatures. 

When the stronghold was finally revealed underneath the refrigerator, the battle had already been won. The colony lay on its dying breath through the work of the poison, and all that was left for me to do was to clean it up. Looking back, I suspect that if I had found the stronghold earlier, I would likely have been paralyzed by fear and unable to continue forward. Instead, encountering it after months of gradual preparation gave me the strength to handle the remains by the time I reached the true source. In that same vein, I find that healing reveals deeper wounds only after we have built the capacity to face them. 

Today, the bait remains in place. Not because the colony is expected to return, but because maintenance is a part of the long-term solution. The work may be done, but the practices that made it possible remain to protect against future undesired invasions. I am deeply amused at the timing of these events and how they have supported my personal growth. In my story, roaches helped me understand the principles of healing. What it will be for you, I have no idea. Regardless, I do think it is worth paying attention to what and how things happen around us, because perhaps then, you may stumble upon more of life's secrets.
