For the past 23 years, I've been an avid Windows lover. In fact, I have loved Windows so much that I spent a lot of my free time being an aggressive Mac hater.

I don't know if I can fully articulate what drew me to Windows. Maybe it was the intuitive UI/UX, the way things were organized, or simply how the operating system evolved over time. Whatever the reason, it felt like home.

But life has a way of moving us towards the unexpected, and I found myself standing at an intersection between idealism and sustainability. Among dentistry, writing, software projects, research, and the growing number of pursuits competing for my attention, I've come to realize that a MacBook best serves the person I am becoming. 

Today, I purchased my first MacBook. It is most definitely not because I have stopped loving Windows, but because I have come to learn that admiration alone is not enough to justify staying. And sometimes, growth requires us to choose what best supports our future over what feels most familiar.

While my path now leads elsewhere, I will continue to watch the evolution of Windows with the same deep affection from the very first time I interacted with it, hoping to witness and celebrate its growth in all the ways I know it is capable of achieving. 

After all, first loves have a way of staying with us, even when they are no longer the place we belong.
