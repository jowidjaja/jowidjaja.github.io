Today, I purchased my first MacBook. For the past 23 years, I've been an avid Windows lover. Always. In fact, I have loved Windows so much that I spent much of the free time of my youth being an aggressive Mac hater.

What drew me to Windows, I don't know if I can fully articulate. Perhaps it was the intuitive UI and UX, the way things were organized, or simply the way the operating system evolved over time. Whatever the reason, it felt like home.

But life has a way of presenting the unexpected, and I've found myself standing at the intersection of idealism and sustainibility. Between dentistry, writing, software projects, research, and the growing number of pursuits competing for my attention, I've come to realize that a MacBook better serves the person I am becoming. 

So, I move forward.

I step into the Mac ecosystem, not because I have stopped loving Windows, but because I've come to learn that admiration alone is not enough to justify staying. Sometimes, growth requires us to choose what best supports our future over what feels most familiar in our past.

While my path now leads elsewhere, I will continue to watch the the evolution of the Windows OS from afar with the same affection from the very first time I interacted with it, hoping to witness and celebrate its growth in all the ways I know it is capable of achieving. 

After all, first loves have a way of staying with us, even when they are no longer where we belong.
